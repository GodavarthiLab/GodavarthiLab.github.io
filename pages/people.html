Dr. Swetha K. Godavarthi
Assistant Professor of Neuroscience
Integrated Sciences Academy, College of Science · Rochester Institute of Technology

Swetha is a cellular and molecular neuroscientist whose work sits at the intersection of synaptic biology, neuroplasticity, and disease. She received her MSc and PhD from the National Brain Research Centre in India, where she studied protein homeostasis in Huntington's disease and Angelman syndrome.

She then joined Nick Spitzer's lab at UC San Diego, where she discovered that postsynaptic receptors retrogradely stabilize presynaptic neurotransmitter identity through transsynaptic bridges — work published in PNAS. She also showed that prenatal stress drives neurotransmitter switching in the prefrontal cortex, causing autism-like behavior in mice. Most recently she was Research Assistant Professor in Steven Meriney's lab at the University of Pittsburgh.

She is a co-PI on an NSF Standard Grant and a Simons Foundation Empire Faculty Fellow. She has mentored more than a dozen undergraduate researchers across her career.
